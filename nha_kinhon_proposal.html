01 Executive Summary & Value Proposition

Nha Kinhon is a technology-first diaspora provisions delivery platform enabling UK-based Guinean expats to order food, hygiene products, and household essentials online and have them physically delivered to family members in Guinea-Bissau within 24 hours. The platform solves a deeply personal, unserved problem: the inability to guarantee that loved ones at home receive essential goods reliably, with full visibility and proof of delivery.

The Nha Kinhon Platform represents a shift from opaque remittance models to a guaranteed-goods ecosystem. Revenue is generated through a combination of product markup and a flat local delivery fee (£2.50) per order, creating sustainable unit economics from day one.

Unlike cash remittance — which offers no guarantee of how money is spent — Nha Kinhon gives the sender complete control and the recipient a tangible benefit.

Strategic Goal: To formalise Phases 1 & 2 via a production build that turns the existing validated prototype into a live, scalable, commercially operational platform connecting the UK corridor to Guinea-Bissau, culminating in public app store deployment.

05 Detailed Portal Workflows

Customer App Workflow

The Customer App is the primary revenue-generating surface. UK-based expats discover products, manage recipients, and complete purchases. The design must build immediate trust with a diaspora audience that has been let down by informal systems — transparent pricing, reliable tracking, and photo proof of delivery are therefore core to every screen.

Users navigate a deliberately two-step checkout to reduce abandonment. They assign a recipient by a unique TRK-XXXX ID, review the itemised order (subtotal + £2.50 local delivery fee), and pay securely via Stripe.

Upon payment, the order is generated in the production backend instantly, and live tracking begins via a visual progress bar indicating state shifts across the ecosystem.

Customer App Workflow

This flow relies heavily on the FastAPI backend returning accurate real-time statuses. When an order transitions to 'Delivered', the app immediately pulls the Proof of Delivery data, showcasing the photo, GPS coordinates, and delivery timestamp directly to the customer.

Agent App Workflow

The operational nerve centre for last-mile delivery in Guinea-Bissau. Built to work reliably in real-world Bissau field conditions — including absent mobile data.

Every feature prioritises agent speed and simplicity. Offline mode is a core reliability requirement, not an edge case. Full data caching ensures assigned orders and manifests are available locally in the Flutter app. Action queues handle delivery confirmations captured when offline, auto-syncing the moment connectivity is restored.

For proof of delivery, the system excludes OTPs intentionally. Instead, the agent captures a photograph of the goods at the location; GPS coordinates are auto-captured at the moment of photo submission — the agent cannot manually enter a location.

Agent App Flow

Once synced, the geo-tagged POD is immediately visible in the Admin Console for geo-fraud cross-check, and all four apps update to 'Delivered' simultaneously. This creates a robust, tamper-evident audit trail for every completed delivery.

Sync Pending Delivered Failed Route
  • Continuous network monitoring and offline action queueing via local caching.
  • Smart-sorted order list keeping the most urgent assigned or in-transit tasks visible.
  • One-tap Call and WhatsApp buttons alongside landmark notes.
  • Standardised failure reporting (Recipient Not Available, Wrong Address, Refused).

14 Security, GDPR & Compliance

Because Nha Kinhon targets a UK-based diaspora and handles financial transactions, the platform is engineered from day one to meet strict European and international compliance standards to build immediate user trust.

Compliance
GDPR by Design
The architecture supports UK Data Protection Act (GDPR) requirements, incorporating data minimization principles and building in automated "Right to Erasure" workflows for end-users.
Payments
Financial Security (PCI-DSS)
We utilize strict Stripe tokenization. Payment details are processed directly via Stripe’s secure iframe; credit card numbers never touch, pass through, or are stored on Nexeor’s backend servers.
Data Storage
Data Encryption
All data in transit is encrypted via TLS/SSL. Sensitive user data and Proof of Delivery coordinates are encrypted at rest within the PostgreSQL database.
